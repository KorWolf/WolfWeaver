Main colors (nearest real) chooses the real image color closest to each cluster center. Main colors (most frequent in cluster) chooses the most common exact color inside each cluster. Main colors (balanced) gently spreads color families without forcing too much diversity. Main colors (diverse) pushes harder for hue-family variety across the selected palette. Main colors (low variety) is meant for simpler images and prioritizes dominant exact colors and clarity. Top frequency uses the most common exact colors overall. Top frequency (low variety) starts from exact source-color frequency and filters near-duplicates for simpler images.

If the image contains fewer unique colors than requested, all unique colors will be used.

Quick Tips

  • Photorealistic images: Use perceptual_lab with clustered_main_balanced. Try 32-64 colors.
  • Stylized / digital art: Use accent_aware with clustered_main_diverse. Lower palettes (16-48) often look cleaner.
  • Limited color / pixel style: Use top_frequency. Keep palette small (8-24 colors).
  • If colors look muddy: Increase palette size or try separation_aware.
  • If edges look noisy: Try coherence_edge_aware.
